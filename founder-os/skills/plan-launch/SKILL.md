# Plan Launch

Building was never the bottleneck — distribution is. This plugin
guides a founder through building excellently and then, without this
skill, drops them at the "now get users" cliff. This skill exists so
that cliff doesn't happen.

## What to do

1. **Confirm the product is actually shippable first.** This skill
   assumes `/ship-checklist` has gone Green at least once already. If
   not, redirect there first — a launch plan for something not yet
   shippable is premature.

2. **Pick exactly one primary channel**, not three. Ask where the target
   users (named back in `/validate-demand`) already spend time, and
   favor a channel the founder can personally show up in (a specific
   subreddit, a specific Slack/Discord community, a specific newsletter,
   direct outreach to a named list) over a generic "post everywhere"
   plan. A weak channel run well beats five channels run thinly.

3. **Write a launch-day playbook as a checklist, not prose**: what gets
   posted/sent, in what order, who (if anyone) gets a heads-up first, and
   what the founder personally does in the first 2 hours after posting
   (reply to every comment, DM the first 10 people who engage — presence
   matters more than reach on day one).

4. **Set one measurable launch-week goal** tied to the job-to-be-done
   from `/validate-demand` — e.g. "10 people complete the core action,"
   not "get traffic." Hand off to `/read-analytics` after launch to
   check it.

5. **Name the follow-up**, explicitly: launch day is the start of
   `/process-feedback`, not the end of the work. Say so before the
   founder mentally checks out post-launch.

## Anti-patterns to avoid

- Planning a multi-channel blitz for a founder's first launch — spread
  too thin, nothing gets a real, attentive push.
- Treating "we launched" as the finish line instead of the start of
  listening to real users.
- Skipping this because the founder is eager to just "post it somewhere"
  — five minutes of channel selection meaningfully changes outcomes.
